Hardwood floor sanding & varnishing in Paris

Floor sanding is the mechanical process of abrading the surface of a solid hardwood floor to remove scratches, stains and irregularities, before applying a protective varnish finish. In Paris, this service is essential for the renovation of Haussmann-era parquet floors — herringbone (point de Hongrie), Versailles pattern, and straight-plank oak — found in thousands of 19th-century apartments.

François Gaillard has been sanding and varnishing hardwood floors in Paris since 2016. Based in Montrouge (92120), adjacent to the 14th arrondissement, he covers all 20 Paris arrondissements and the entire Île-de-France region.

The HTC planetary sanding machine

François Gaillard uses the HTC Husqvarna planetary sanding machine exclusively — three satellite discs working simultaneously in all directions. This machine is indispensable for diagonal patterns (herringbone, Versailles) where drum sanders leave visible directional marks. The result is perfectly homogeneous, with no marks regardless of the floor pattern.

Bona Mega Evo varnish — 3 coats

All floors are finished with 3 coats of Bona Mega Evo — a professional water-based varnish certified EMICODE EC1PLUS and GREENGUARD Gold (low VOC, safe for children and pets). Non-yellowing, 10-15 year lifespan. Re-entry 8 hours after the final coat.
